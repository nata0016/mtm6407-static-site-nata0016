# Gear Guide

<section class="page-section">
  <h2>Essential Riding Equipment</h2>
  <p>
    Good gear doesn’t need to be expensive, but it needs to be safe, durable, and properly fitted.
    The right equipment makes your rides more comfortable and helps your horse move freely.
  </p>
</section>

<section class="page-section">
  <h2>Helmets & Safety Gear</h2>
  <p>
    A certified, well-fitted helmet is non-negotiable. Look for MIPS technology, ventilation, and proper harness fit.
  </p>

  <div class="info-card">
    <h3>Fit Tip</h3>
    <p>
      Your helmet should not move when you shake your head. If it slides, it's too big.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="page-section">
  <h2>Saddles & Pads</h2>
  <p>
    A saddle should be fitted by a professional to avoid discomfort or long-term injury.
    Saddle pads help protect the horse’s back and absorb shock.
  </p>

  <div class="info-card">
    <h3>What to Look For</h3>
    <p>
      Balanced tree, even panel contact, correct gullet width, and no pinching behind the shoulders.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="page-section">
  <h2>Breeches, Boots & Gloves</h2>
  <p>
    Rider apparel should allow freedom of movement while providing grip and stability.
  </p>

  <div class="info-card">
    <h3>Favorite Picks</h3>
    <p>
      • Silicone-grip breeches<br>
      • Tall boots with flexible ankle<br>
      • Breathable gloves for grip without sweating
    </p>
  </div>
</section>
